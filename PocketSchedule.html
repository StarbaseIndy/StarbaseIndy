Astrophysics Sherlock Holmes and the Beginning of Modern Forensic Science with David Zauner.

Arthur Conan Doyle's character, Sherlock Holmes, was the first fictional detective to explicitly base his solutions of cases on observation, science, and deductive reasoning. Many of the stories include accounts of Holmes' detailed examinations of crime scenes and pieces of evidence. David Zauner, a member of the Indianapolis Sherlock Holmes society, The Illustrious Clients, and a retired forensic scientist, will explore how the Holmes stories reflect applications of scientific principles to criminal investigations in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries and how forensic science has developed since that time to its present state.
HoloCenter Friendship is Magic: Bronies 4 Life a My Little Pony Discussion Panel with Emily Rumeau and PJ O'Conner

One might wonder what one of the most intellectual and influential science-fiction shows in history could possibly have in common with a colorful, animated children's program populated with a character cast of tiny equines. Well, today's panel will include a discussion of the many similarities between the most recent version of My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic and the many series of Star Trek in regards to aspects such as themes, character development, and script writing. We will also cover the similarities between the fandoms and the user-generated content these two shows inspire fans to create. Joining us as experts on creative content in both the Trek and MLP fandoms are several members of the Shake-ups in Ponyville! An Indianapolis-based MLP fandom band which, for the past several years, has been producing and performing original music about the show at events all around the country. They were recently voted "Best Local Pop Group" by Nuvo Magazine for 2016, and their lead singer, Patrick O'Connor is also a member of the Star Trek fandom's premier rock band, Five Year Mission.

7:00pm Astrophysics Consent & Cuddles with Lisa Meece

After several years of running the Partying Safely panel at conventions, Lisa realized that the ability to manage one's own boundaries is the foundational element of staying safe. Whether it's consenting to physical touch or consenting to that next alcoholic drink, the principles involved (and the skills required to do it effectively) remain the same. Consent is a popular topic of discussion, and there's some reason to believe it's not well understood throughout popular culture. Physical touch provides an excellent workshop tool for practicing how to give, deny, and revoke consent - and how to manage your feelings when others do those things in relation to you. We'll take a very "training wheels" approach in the session, and then those who consent will have some time to cuddle with each other.

Astrophysics To the Journey: Outer Space and Inner Space in Star Trek with Dr. Mary Jo Weaver

A Buddhist mantra says "breathing in I see myself as space/breathing out I feel free." Dr. Weaver expand on that idea by imagining "space" as inviting adventure both exhilarating and sometimes frightening or mysterious. She will explore the concept of the inner journey as it evolves in Trek, a drama about outer space. Trek's variations on the concept of journey suggests that it is closer to the dynamic possibilities of religious mystery than traditional doctrine and practice . Think about this line from an 8th century text about a relationship with the divine: "Thou are the journey and the journey's end." Then remember Trek episodes that take us to the end of time or space or explainable reality.
